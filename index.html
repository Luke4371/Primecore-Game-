Strategic Plan 2024–2028

“What is creativity? It is a reaction. It's a conversation. It's a dance ... It's beautiful to see.” – Amelie Prone, Young Critic at the Crawford Art Gallery for Youth Theatre Ireland

Cork ETB’s Local Creative Youth Partnership (LCYP) is a youth-led network that expands creative opportunities for children and young people aged 0–24 across Cork city and county.

Transformational Power of Creativity

Welcome to the first Strategic Plan for Cork ETB's Local Creative Youth Partnership (LCYP). Established in April 2022, the Cork LCYP is, at its core, a network to enable information sharing and collaboration between the ETB, local authorities, the non-formal education sector, family support services and youth services.

Directly funded by government and led by the voices of young people, the LCYP aims to explore and develop pathways to ensure that all children and young people can flourish creatively, delivering opportunities for experimentation, play and creative engagement.

Creativity is described as fluid, intangible, delightful and innovative, supporting the social, personal and creative development of young people and enabling relationship-building, collaboration and the exchange of ideas.

The aims of the partnership are to give every child and young person (0–24 years) in Cork city and county practical access to out-of-school tuition, experience and participation in creativity. Young people's participation in decision-making is central to the development, implementation and evaluation of plans and activities of the LCYP.

Finding Our Voice

Cork ETB Local Creative Youth Partnership has created a pilot youth-led forum called the Rebel Youth Creatives (RYC) to consult on creative matters. Through monthly meetings, young people have participated in creating a youth-informed strategic plan in a warm, welcoming environment that builds confidence and self-esteem.

One member describes the impact: “As someone who comes from an area of rural isolation, this opportunity has been life-changing for me as I have made incredible friends and connections.”

The LCYP ensures all voices are heard, especially those of young people who are seldom heard. Diversity and inclusion are at the core of the Partnership and the Rebel Youth Creatives group, implementing Article 12 of the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child using the Lundy Model.

“As a young person, being a Rebel Youth Creative has been incredibly exciting and invaluable to my career as an emerging artist and of course as a young person. I have such a clear understanding of my rights: I have found my voice.” – Katie O’Rourke, Rebel Youth Creative

Background and Context

The Creative Youth Plan, published in December 2017, aims to give every child practical access to tuition, experience and participation in creativity and to ensure that children and young people retain a key voice in decision-making, prioritising those most at risk of disadvantage.

Creativity is described as “the use of imaginative capabilities to transform thinking and produce original and innovative ideas and solutions.”

Creative Youth & Creative Ireland Programme

The Creative Ireland Programme is an all-of-government, culture-based initiative that emphasises the importance of human creativity for individual, community and national wellbeing. It is grounded in the belief that every person has creative capacities, the development of which contributes to personal and collective wellbeing.

The long-term objective is that wellbeing is enhanced through increased participation in creative activity at all stages of life. Key partners in delivering Creative Youth include the Department of Education (DoE), the Department of Children, Equality, Disability, Integration and Youth (DCEDIY), and the Department of Tourism, Culture, Arts, Gaeltacht, Sport and Media (DTCAGSM).

The Creative Youth Plan intends to enable and propagate the creative potential of every child and young person aged 0–24 through expanded and new initiatives, creating a place where knowledge and creativity are equal partners in forming creative, active citizens.

Cork ETB – Uniquely Placed to Deliver LCYP

Cork ETB is the ideal host platform to deliver the Local Creative Youth Partnership as it is uniquely placed to engage young people who may be seldom-heard or hard-to-reach. It promotes a culture of growth and development in which children and young people are encouraged to give full expression to their talents and gifts.

Cork ETB has a strong school network including national schools, post-primary schools and colleges, as well as 12 Youthreach centres across the region. Its Arts Education Strategy underpins a commitment to building a creative and culturally-aware organisation.

Cork ETB also coordinates 30 staffed youth work projects and services and over 170 volunteer-led clubs. An evaluation of the initial pilot LCYPs identified the success of positioning LCYP within the youth work sections of ETBs.

Consultations on the LCYP Plan

The Strategic Plan is founded on extensive consultations, conversations and evaluations of Cork’s 2022–2023 pilot LCYP. It is aligned with Cork ETB’s Strategic Plan, Arts Education Strategy, Youth Services Strategic Plan, European Youth Goals, the Arts Council’s Equality, Human Rights and Diversity policy, and local authority Arts & Culture strategies.

The LCYP is situated within Cork ETB Youth Services and supported by Hub na nÓg, fitting with the government’s Young Ireland National Policy Framework for Children and Young People 2023–2028.

Consultations with hundreds of children and young people across Cork identified key areas of need:

  • Space
  • Awareness
  • Creative collaboration
  • Skills development
  • Diversity and inclusion
  • Access
  • Rights-based approach

Young people expressed strong interest in ongoing engagement, with 80 young people interested in joining an LCYP youth forum to inform the work.

Mission Statement & Priorities

Mission Statement

Cork LCYP develops local networks with a wide range of organisations to expand and provide inclusive, diverse and responsive creative opportunities for all children and young people aged 0–24 in out-of-school settings, with a particular focus on those who are marginalised or who experience disadvantage in the Cork ETB functional area.

Core Values and Guiding Principles – Priorities

  • Engage children and young people aged 0–24, with a special focus on those aged 10+.
  • Engage children and young people in Cork city and county, with a special focus on seldom-heard voices.
  • Build a sustainable network of organisations and practitioners to provide practical, participatory creative opportunities.
  • Build capacity on personal, social and organisational levels to enhance the aims of Cork LCYP.
  • Ensure that the voice of the child is front and foremost in creative project planning, taking their views seriously and working collaboratively.

From Paper to Practice – Objectives & Actions

Objective 1: Be Youth-Led

Show actions ▶
  • Deliver training on the Lundy “Everyday Spaces” checklist.
  • Develop the dedicated youth forum, the Rebel Youth Creatives.
  • Establish diverse focus groups for ongoing youth consultation.
  • Support creative workshops in response to needs identified by young people.
  • Trial innovative approaches for seldom-heard young people to access creative opportunities.
  • Ensure access to open, welcoming spaces that celebrate the arts and diversity.
  • Support young people to be creative leaders and progress to further learning and creative careers.

Objective 2: Develop the Partnership

Show actions ▶
  • Connect venues and artists with youth organisations.
  • Build awareness through public showcases and events.
  • Develop a social media presence supported by young people.
  • Create actions in areas of rural isolation, with a focus on West Cork.
  • Build on relationships with Cork ETB FET, Youthreach and Youth Services to develop progression routes.
  • Identify local partners and bridge gaps in provision.
  • Offer training and CPD opportunities for personal, social and artistic growth in Youth Arts.
  • Ensure a collaborative approach that avoids duplication and responds to young people’s voices.

Objective 3: Foster Inclusion

Show actions ▶
  • Share best practice.
  • Measure the impact of creative youth work.
  • Ensure diverse programming reflecting different cultural contexts and participants’ experiences.
  • Create awareness of opportunities.
  • Support emerging young artists to access arts facilitation training and employment.
  • Provide inclusive role models.
  • Recognise the value of universal provision for integrating diverse young people.
  • Target young people with seldom-heard voices.
  • Embed social inclusion into initiatives.

Objective 4: Deepen Creative Collaborations

Show actions ▶
  • Support groups to engage with creative themes identified by young people: Climate & Creativity, Music & Migration, Youth Media.
  • Support Erasmus+ applications to increase breadth and scope of engagement.
  • Build Cork ETB Youth Services’ international engagement with Erasmus+.
  • Support creatives to engage with multi-agencies on programme development.
  • Create new initiatives and develop existing provision.
  • Support creative actions in the Irish language, recognising the importance of digital and creative skills for young people as they celebrate their achievements through inspiring workshops.
